Sollu is for busy families

Who want to be more "involved" but dont know how

We all have values and things we believe in, and we all want to give back to make the world around us a better place. But we are busy, we have kids, and we don’t always know where to make those values real. Sollu makes it possible for all of us - of different backgrounds, ages, abilities, to actualize our moral and ethical teachings to impact and uplift the world.

What does Sollu do?

Sollu makes it possible for busy people like you to do meaningful, good work in the world. We offer family friendly service opportunities that help the vulnerable, collection drives, visits to those in need, and more. Through these hands-on experiences, we change the world and we teach our values to the next generation.

What Does Sollu Mean?

Where does the word Sollu come from?

“Sollu Sollu!” These are the famous words read on Yom Kippur by Jewish communities across the globe. They come from the Haftorah, a selection from the Book of Isaiah, and they mean “pave a road,” or “make a way.”

The instruction by the prophet to the people is to clear the obstacles that stand between the people and their highest moral and spiritual potential.

The Haftorah then goes on to challenge the Jewish people, reminding them that their ritual observance is meaningless to God if they are not helping the poor, the homeless, and the vulnerable around them.

We are inspired by this message and seek to live it out today; bridging the gap between ritual and ethics and making it possible for all to live out our highest calling as Jews.

Uplifting

Our work helps others, but it also helps bring us closer together. We extend beyond the artificial borders of our own communities and use hands-on immersive experiences bring positive change to greater community.

Not just words - ACTION!

Sollu is committed to doing! At every Sollu event there will be hands on ways for children and adults to tangibly make a difference in the lives of others around them. We will also find time for reflection and learning, but in Sollu, doing is primary.

Inclusive

Sollu is powered by ancient Jewish wisdom and tradition and welcomes participation by all - no matter the background, age, faith, or no faith. In Sollu we believe that coming together to do good unites us all.
